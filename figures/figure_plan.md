# Figure Plan

Target reference: the clear, sparse visual language of "Reasoning with Sampling: Your Base Model is Smarter Than You Think", with EBT-level mechanism clarity.

## Main Figures

1. Hero concept: a four-step strip showing dream future, local body test, residual diagnosis, and repaired executable future.
2. Learned-J interface: one learned local action map supports inverse action, failure diagnosis, and future repair.
3. Environment atlas: four compact case cards showing why plausible futures can fail under different embodiments.
4. Hostile baselines: sparse grouped bars, not a dense horizontal ranking chart.
5. Robustness audit: three clear bar panels for analytic vs learned maps, noise stress, and wrong-map exposure.
6. Repair storyboard: before/after trajectory cards with execution-gap reduction callouts.
7. Embodiment swap: warm residual fields with a non-black low-energy palette and shared colorbar.
8. Visual/keypoint futures: separated keypoint examples plus grouped visual-score bars.

## Acceptance Bar

- No dense long bar chart in the main paper.
- No overlapping panels or labels.
- All figures use the same palette, type scale, line widths, and panel rhythm.
- Quantitative panels should be readable at 50 percent zoom.
- Qualitative panels should communicate the mechanism before the caption is read.
