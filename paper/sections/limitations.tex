\section{Limitations}

The benchmark is still controlled and CPU-only. It tests local representation-space feasibility, not real robot reliability. The repair solver is a projected-inference routine, not a full nonlinear optimal-control solver. The residual is local and can be misleading when second-order effects, contact discontinuities, perception errors, or long-horizon compounding dominate. Learned Jacobians are evaluated with small local regressors rather than neural Jacobian fields. The pusher exposes the central failure mode: contact discontinuities can break a smooth local map unless the robot actively probes the contact mode or uses a mode-aware representation. These limitations are intentional for a first mechanism paper, but real robot or stronger simulator validation would be required before deployment claims.
