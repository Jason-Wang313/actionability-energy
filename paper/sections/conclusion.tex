\section{Conclusion}

Actionability energy formalizes a simple requirement for embodied world models: imagined representation changes should lie near the local action-reachable tangent set of the body that must execute them. In controlled CPU benchmarks, analytic and learned local Jacobian maps predict execution failure, support repair, and change under embodiment swaps. The result is not a replacement for video planners, inverse dynamics, or real robot evaluation. It reframes Jacobian-style embodiment maps as diagnostic interfaces for world-model planning: they help a robot ask whether the future it imagined is actually actionable by its own body.
